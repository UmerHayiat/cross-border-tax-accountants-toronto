Understanding Cross-Border Taxation

The Complexities of U.S.-Canada Tax Laws

Cross-border taxation involves two distinct systems. The U.S. practices citizenship-based taxation, requiring citizens and green card holders to report worldwide income. Canadian businesses face the challenge of navigating U.S. federal, state, and local tax rules.

Common Challenges

  • U.S. Expatriates in Canada: Compliance with both Canadian and U.S. tax obligations, including Form 1040 and FATCA disclosures.
  • Canadian Businesses Operating in the U.S.: Dual reporting for Canadian and U.S. tax requirements, sales tax compliance, and transfer pricing.
  • Real Estate Investors: Withholding taxes, capital gains taxes, and rental income compliance.

Managing cross-border taxation without professional guidance can result in mistakes or missed savings opportunities.
